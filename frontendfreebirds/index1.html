Vocational Agriculture Courses After 10th Class


What is vocational agriculture?

Vocational agriculture trains people for jobs in such areas as production, marketing, and conservation. College agriculture involves training of people to teach or conduct research in order to advance the fields of agriculture and food science.

Agriculture is defined as the practice of cultivating the land or raising stock. Agriculture courses in India are helpful in understanding these practices and putting them into practice. Agriculture education in India is a specialised one, provided by agriculture universities in India. Indian Council of Agricultural Research (ICAR) is the apex body for coordinating, guiding and managing research and education in Agriculture including Horticulture, Fisheries and Animal Sciences in India. ICAR, New Delhi, conducts All India Entrance Exam to fill 15% seats in all Agriculture Universities all over India. Indian economy is largely an agriculture based one. Careers in Agriculture thus become relevant to employment. Agriculture professionals are known as Agriculturist or Agriculturalist. Agriculturists are specialists in all areas of cultivation and raising livestock. They are also referred as Agriculture Scientists as agriculture itself is a science. As per their designations they advise farmers on soil management, breeding animals, crop protection, disease, harvesting and other concerned aspects. Apart from theoretical knowledge, agriculture syllabi also includes research in a variety of areas that directly impact the food and agriculture industry. Agriculture is a vast field and its sub fields include horticulture, dairying, poultry farming and so on.


Eligibility Criteria

  • The student must have completed class 10th from a school that comes under a recognised board of education.
  • The aggregate score of the candidate in class 10th board exams . Some relaxation in aggregate marks is provided at most of the colleges to the students belonging to reserved categories
  • The candidate must have received passing marks in all the subjects of class 10th.
  • The time duration is two years

Benefits of Vocational Agricultural Course

To understand the critical importance of agriculture and allied areas. To develop basic knowledge on the elements and practices of agriculture. Formulating national policies, schemes and programmes for achieving rapid growth through the optimum utilisation of land, water, soil and plant resources. To bring about the proper development of marketing of agricultural produce to safeguard the economic interests of the farming community.
