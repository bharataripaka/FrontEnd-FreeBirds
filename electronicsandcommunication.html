Diploma in Electronics and Communication



Diploma in Electronics and Communication Engineering is a Diploma level Electronics and Communication Engineering course. The focus in this discipline is on understanding electronic networks and devices, electric magnetic fields, computer fundamentals, communication and control systems. Electronics and Communication Engineering uses the scientific knowledge of the behaviour and effects of electronics to develop components, devices, systems and equipment.

Electronics and Communication Engineering is considered as the backbone of the modern communication system. In the current scenario, modern applications like the Internet of Things (IoT), Robotics, and Embedded Systems are highly dependent on the knowledge of ECE. One of the prime objectives of an ECE program is to equip students with necessary core competency in order to succeed in life after completing the sameStudents enrolled in this course are taught to direct, balance, control and test production processes, ensure safety, installation and proper functioning of the various mechanisms, understand and apply digital electronics.


Eligibility criteria

  • The student must have completed class 10th from a school that comes under a recognised board of education.
  • The aggregate score of the candidate in class 10th board exams must be at least 50%. Some relaxation in aggregate marks is provided at most of the colleges to the students belonging to reserved categories
  • The candidate must have received passing marks in all the subjects of class 10th.
  • The time duration is three years

Board

To help speed up the process of development of Technical Education, a Board in the name and style of "STATE BOARD OF TECHNICAL EDUCATION AND TRAINING" was created vide G.O.Ms.No.371 Edn-dated 26.2.1957. The Joint Director (Technical Education) of DPI used to act as secretary of this newly formed Board. The Board is formed as a statutory body under the A.P. Education Act-1982 (act 1 of 1982), an act of assembly, in the present form, vide G.O.Ms.No.140 dated 24.4.1984.
